# ERC-4626 Tokenized Vault Standard

## Part 1: How UltraFast uses ERC-4626 and why

UltraFast is a pre-implementation Layer 1 (perp futures, scalar prediction markets, data sales, EVM lane) being designed by the MANTRA team. The Community Vault described in §9.7 of the whitepaper is the protocol-owned-liquidity primitive, and it is multi-strategy from day 1 rather than monolithic. The whitepaper states explicitly: "Hyperliquid's HLP is the existence proof that a monolithic vault scales to roughly \$500M before its risk-isolation properties bind." HLP today sits in the $300–500M range and its 12-month CAGR has compressed from a 42% lifetime figure to about 22%, consistent with the diminishing-returns argument UltraFast uses to justify segmentation. Each UltraFast strategy is a separate contract implementing `deposit`, `withdraw`, and `reportPnL`. Initial strategies are volatility-targeted market making, basis arbitrage (perp versus spot or perp versus prediction-market), and a liquidation backstop. Additional strategies may be deployed by governance or third parties post-launch.

Share semantics are ERC-4626-style transferable claims per strategy. A vault-of-vaults wrapper offers retail depositors a blended exposure across the active strategies. Per-strategy lock windows govern redemption cadence (volatility-targeted: daily; backstop: weekly), and per-strategy capacity caps route surplus deposits to the next-best strategy via the configured allocator.

The reasons for adopting the ERC-4626 interface rather than a bespoke vault ABI are mostly composability and security:

- The standard is the dominant yield-bearing-vault interface on EVM. Anything that integrates ERC-4626 — DeFi aggregators, lending markets that accept share tokens as collateral, structured-product wrappers, accounting tooling — works against UltraFast strategy shares with no custom adapter. Aggregate ERC-4626/7540 vault TVL across the major issuers (Morpho, Yearn V3, Sky, Spark, Pendle, Ethena, Origin, plus tokenized-treasury wrappers) sits around $25B as of April 2026.
- Transferable shares give the strategies a secondary market. A depositor exiting a weekly-locked backstop strategy can sell the share token rather than wait for the lock window.
- The standard is audit-mature. The reference OpenZeppelin implementation has been hardened against the inflation attack discussed in Part 2, and external auditors are fluent in the failure modes.
- The vault-of-vaults wrapper composes naturally: it is itself an ERC-4626 vault whose underlying assets are other ERC-4626 shares. This mirrors the MetaMorpho pattern.

Risk-isolation between strategies is a property of the contract topology, not of the share standard itself: ERC-4626 says nothing about whether one vault can cascade losses into another. UltraFast enforces isolation by giving each strategy its own collateral account and forbidding cross-strategy rehypothecation. ERC-4626 is the share-accounting layer on top of that isolation.

## Part 2: Deep research on ERC-4626

### Specification and provenance

ERC-4626, defined by EIP-4626, was finalised in March–April 2022. Authors of record are Joey Santoro (Fei Protocol founder), t11s / Transmissions11 (Rari Capital), Jet Jadeja, Alberto Cuesta Cañada (Yield Protocol, later OpenZeppelin), and Mr Toledo / Señor Doggo. The motivating problem was that pre-2022 every yield protocol — Yearn, Compound, Aave, Idle, Harvest, Rari, Convex — had invented its own deposit/withdraw/share-price ABI, and aggregators had to write a bespoke adapter per protocol. ERC-4626 unified the interface.

### How it works

An ERC-4626 vault is itself an ERC-20 token. Its balance represents a proportional claim on a single underlying ERC-20 asset (the "asset"). The standard mandates a small set of functions:

- `asset()` returns the underlying ERC-20 address.
- `totalAssets()` returns the total amount of underlying asset under management, including accrued yield.
- `convertToShares(assets)` and `convertToAssets(shares)` are the pricing primitives. They use the formula `shares = assets × totalSupply / totalAssets` (and the inverse).
- `deposit(assets, receiver)` and `mint(shares, receiver)` mint shares against deposited asset.
- `withdraw(assets, receiver, owner)` and `redeem(shares, receiver, owner)` burn shares against returned asset.
- `previewDeposit`, `previewMint`, `previewWithdraw`, `previewRedeem` are off-chain pricing views that MUST match the on-chain action exactly under current state.
- `maxDeposit`, `maxMint`, `maxWithdraw`, `maxRedeem` advertise per-receiver/per-owner caps.

Share price is therefore not stored — it is derived on every call from `totalAssets()` and `totalSupply`. Yield accrues by `totalAssets()` increasing without a corresponding mint, which raises the implicit price of every outstanding share.

### Production users

Production adoption in 2026 is broad. The headline implementers:

- **Yearn V3** (launched 2023) replaced Yearn V2's bespoke deposit/withdraw with full ERC-4626 compliance. All Yearn V3 vaults — yvUSDC, yvWETH, yvDAI, the curated strategy vaults — are 4626 tokens.
- **Morpho Blue** (deployed early 2024) exposes each isolated lending market as an ERC-4626 vault. MetaMorpho vaults are ERC-4626 wrappers that allocate across multiple Morpho Blue markets. As of January 2026, Morpho vaults attract the majority of net new deposits in the vault sector.
- **Aave V3** ships a Stata wrapper (Static aToken) that wraps a rebasing aToken into a 4626-compliant non-rebasing share token. The base aToken is not natively 4626 because of its rebasing accounting.
- **Sommelier Cellars** are ERC-4626 vaults; the protocol describes its Cellars as hewing close to the standard.
- **Pendle** is not 4626 at the principal-token (PT) or standardised-yield (SY) layer, but it accepts 4626 vaults as input assets, so any 4626 share token can be deposited into Pendle and stripped into yield and principal.
- **Maple Finance**, **Origin**, **Ethena**, **Sky/Spark** lending vaults, **Lagoon**, and **Kamino** (on Solana, via the Anchor analogue) all ship 4626 or 4626-compatible vault tokens.
- **Rocket Pool** rETH and **Lido** stETH/wstETH predate the standard and are not strictly 4626, though wstETH is sometimes described as 4626-like because it is a non-rebasing share against an internally appreciating asset.
- BlackRock BUIDL and other tokenized-treasury wrappers route through 4626 or 7540 wrappers when they appear in DeFi venues.

DefiLlama and the 4626 Alliance registry track 1,300+ vaults across these protocols.

### Variants and extensions

- **ERC-7540 (Asynchronous ERC-4626 Tokenized Vaults)** was finalised in March 2024. It extends 4626 to support deposits and redemptions that do not settle in the same transaction. Requests pass through three states — Pending, Claimable, Claimed — separating the user's request from the vault's fulfilment. This is essential for RWA protocols where the underlying asset settles T+N: custody transfers, NAV attestation, bank-wire confirmation, SPV-signed proofs. Centrifuge, UltraYield, and Lagoon are production users. The 4626 sync interface is preserved as a fallback.
- **ERC-7575 (Multi-Asset Vaults)** externalises the ERC-20 share-token implementation from the 4626 logic so a single share token can be issued in exchange for multiple different underlying assets. It is the standard used together with 7540 for multi-asset RWA vaults. Contracts implementing the async vault standard MUST implement the 7575 `share` method.
- **Epoch-based withdrawals**: a deployment pattern (Maple, several lending vaults) where redemptions are batched into discrete epochs. Pre-7540 this was implemented as an extension on top of 4626; post-7540 such designs typically migrate to the async standard.
- **Virtual-shares offset** (OpenZeppelin) and **dead-share** patterns are not separate EIPs but are now standard implementation defences against the inflation attack below.

### Comparison to pre-2022 ad-hoc vault designs

Before ERC-4626, Yearn V2 used `pricePerShare`, Compound used `exchangeRateStored`, Aave V2's aTokens rebased balances, Idle had its own per-strategy ABI, Rari had multiple. An aggregator routing between them needed a bespoke adapter per protocol, and a share token from one protocol could not be used as collateral in another without that protocol writing a custom pricing oracle. The 4626 standard collapsed this fragmentation: one ABI, one set of audit patterns, one set of integrations.

### Common pitfalls

- **Share inflation / first-depositor / donation attack**: the canonical 4626 pitfall. An attacker is the first depositor into a fresh vault, deposits 1 wei of asset, and receives 1 share. They then `transfer` (not `deposit`) a large quantity — say 10,000 USDC — of the underlying asset directly into the vault, bypassing the mint logic. `totalAssets()` now reads ~10,000 USDC against a `totalSupply` of 1 share. A victim depositing 5,000 USDC receives `5,000 × 1 / 10,000 = 0` shares after rounding down. The attacker holds the only share, now backed by 15,000 USDC, and can redeem the whole balance. A 2023 incident on a smaller Sherlock-style vault lost roughly $200,000 to this exact pattern, and pre-deployment audits caught the same bug on Sommelier and Idle.
- **Rounding direction**: 4626 specifies which direction each function MUST round (deposits and previews round down for shares, withdrawals round up for assets consumed). Implementations that round the wrong way leak value to the attacker on every interaction.
- **Share-price manipulation via oracle dependencies**: if `totalAssets()` reads from an externally manipulable oracle (e.g. a spot AMM price), an attacker can sandwich deposits and redemptions. Mitigation: TWAP or internal accounting only.
- **Reentrancy through callbacks**: if the vault calls an underlying strategy that calls back into the vault before share accounting is settled, share supply can be inflated. Standard `nonReentrant` discipline applies.

### Defensive patterns

- **Virtual shares and assets** (OpenZeppelin reference implementation): the vault behaves as if it permanently holds an extra `10^decimalsOffset` virtual shares and virtual assets, decoupling the share-price math from the real `totalSupply`. The attacker can no longer drive the share price arbitrarily high with a 1-wei deposit.
- **Dead-share pattern**: the deployer's first deposit mints a non-trivial share quantity to the zero address (or a burn address), locking permanent value into the vault and bounding the inflation factor. Aave Stata uses a variation.
- **Minimum first deposit**: some vaults require the very first deposit to be above a minimum size, eliminating the 1-wei attack vector at the cost of UX.
- **Trusted seeding**: the deployer seeds the vault with a known non-trivial amount before opening to the public.

### Aggregator and wrapper support

DEX and yield aggregators — 1inch, ParaSwap, CowSwap, Yearn vault routers, Morpho's meta-vault layer — treat 4626 vaults as first-class. Lending protocols accept 4626 share tokens as collateral by reading `convertToAssets(balanceOf(user))` for valuation. Accounting tools (DeBank, Zapper, Zerion) parse 4626 ABIs natively. This integration density is what UltraFast inherits by issuing strategy shares under the standard rather than a bespoke ABI.
